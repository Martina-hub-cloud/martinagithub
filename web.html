Rotoscopy is an animation technique that animators use to trace over motion picture footage,
frame by frame, to produce realistic action.
Originally, animators projected photographed
live-action movie images onto a glass panel and traced over the image.
This projection equipment is referred to as a rotoscope,
developed by Polish-American animator Max Fleischer.
This device was eventually replaced by computers,
but the process is still called rotoscoping.
